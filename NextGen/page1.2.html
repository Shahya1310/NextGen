Collection of Bagasse

Bagasse is the fibrous residue left after sugarcane is crushed to extract the juice. This waste material is collected from sugar mills.

pulp creation

The soaked bagasse is then pulped, typically by mechanical or chemical methods, to break it down into fibres

Shaping and Molding:

The pulp is moulded into plate-shaped forms using high-pressure machinery, and excess water is drained.

Drying

The moulded plates are dried to remove any remaining moisture
